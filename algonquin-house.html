- Algonquin House -

Whiteface Club Resort

The Algonquin House is a gorgeous custom built Adirondack 'rustic style' home situated on a private lot overlooking the golf course and mountains at Whiteface Club Resort.

The home features five bedrooms, two master suites, 4.5 baths, Great room with soaring ceilings, stone fireplace(gas) fully equipped kitchen with granite counter tops and stainless steel appliances. Adjoining the dining area is the screened in room with a second fireplace (wood burning) which walks out to the back deck sitting area and grill.

Bedrooms include a 1st floor master bedroom with king bed and private bath featuring a jetted tub, stone walk in shower and double sinks. Open log stairway with walk around open balcony leads to a second master bedroom-king bed, private bath, queen guest bedroom, bunk room with a twin over double bunk and a shared bath. A small office area in the open balcony allows guest a computer for their use.

The finished lower level offers a huge game room with Foosball table, sitting area two double beds-all open and an enclosed bedroom with two twin beds and a full bath.

Guest will enter the home into a huge mud room/entry way for throwing off shoes and winter coats, skis, ect., with utility room, half bath and connected one car garage;

All guest will receive free passes to use the swimming beach at the resort with all additional amenities on a fee basis...golf, tennis, marina, cross country skiing, snowshoeing(rentals available) and two restaurants.

The Adirondacks offer so many opportunities to experience the great outdoors. Numerous nearby lakes and rivers provide our guests with swimming, rafting, boating, kayaking, SUP, canoeing, and fishing. The mountains offer hiking, rock climbing, ice climbing, and of course, winter skiing. Additionally, there are opportunities for horseback riding, snow shoeing, cross country skiing and summer hiking and camping. There are many guide services and ski shops that can help you plan your outdoor events and can rent you all the equipment you need. There is also plenty to do if you are not the "outdoorsy" type: stores, gift shops, theaters, art galleries, book stores, yarn shops, farm markets, Olympic Museums, and stunning road trip opportunities. Inside the Algonquin House you will find a notebook that will give you some of my personal suggestions for hiking, restaurants along with your checking out instructions.
